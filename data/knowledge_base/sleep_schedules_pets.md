# Sleep Schedules for Common Pets

Sleep needs vary dramatically by species, age, and individual. Mismatched expectations cause needless worry — many "lethargic" calls to the vet are simply a pet sleeping a normal amount.

## Daily sleep totals (approximate)

- Adult dogs: 12–14 hours
- Puppies and senior dogs: 18–20 hours
- Adult cats: 14–16 hours
- Kittens and senior cats: 18–20 hours
- Rabbits: 8 hours, in short bursts; mostly active dawn and dusk
- Parrots: 10–12 hours of dark, undisturbed sleep — under-slept parrots scream and pluck

## Sleep environment

- Provide 1–2 dedicated sleeping spots per pet, in different rooms
- Beds should be away from foot-traffic but visible enough to feel "in the pack"
- Senior pets benefit from orthopedic foam beds — joint stiffness is worse on hard surfaces
- Cats need elevated options as well as ground options
- Birds need full darkness and quiet; a covered cage in a guest bedroom works better than the family room

## Disturbance patterns

Dogs and cats both run a 90-minute sleep cycle. Avoid scheduling tasks that interrupt the same window every day, or you fragment their sleep.

## When to worry

Sudden changes are more meaningful than absolute amounts:
- A normally-active dog now sleeping 18+ hours: see a vet
- A senior cat suddenly wakeful at night: possible cognitive issue or hyperthyroidism — vet
- Restless sleep, twitching beyond normal dreaming, vocalizing: noteworthy if new

## Schedule design implications

- Schedule the last potty break / litter check 30–60 min before owner's bedtime to align cycles
- Avoid heavy exercise in the 90 minutes before sleep — dogs especially won't settle
- Cats need an evening "hunt" play session followed by a small meal — this is the natural settle pattern

The PawPal+ scheduler doesn't optimize for sleep directly, but a well-designed daily routine respects the pet's sleep needs by leaving long uninterrupted windows for rest.

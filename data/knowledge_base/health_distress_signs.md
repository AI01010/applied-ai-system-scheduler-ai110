# Recognizing Signs of Distress in Pets

Pets hide illness — it is an evolutionary holdover. A pet who shows obvious distress is often already past the early stages. Owners who know their pet's normal behavior catch problems faster than any algorithm.

## Universal signs that warrant a same-day vet call

- Refusing food for over 24 hours (cats: 12 hours — cats can develop hepatic lipidosis quickly when not eating)
- Drinking dramatically more or less than usual
- Vomiting more than twice in a day, or any vomiting that contains blood
- Diarrhea lasting over 24 hours, or any bloody stool
- Lethargy that persists through the day (not just "sleepy")
- Hiding for over 24 hours (cats especially)
- Limping or favoring a limb
- Difficulty breathing — open-mouth breathing in cats is always an emergency
- Pale gums (should be pink and moist)
- Distended abdomen
- Inability to urinate (cats: blocked urethra is fatal within 24–48 hours)

## Behavioral changes to take seriously

- Sudden aggression in a normally friendly pet
- Loss of house-training in a previously trained pet
- Excessive vocalization at night (often pain-related in seniors)
- Confusion or "lost" behavior in familiar spaces
- Sudden weight loss (>10% body weight) over weeks

## What PawPal+ does and does not do

The scheduler tracks tasks. It does not monitor the pet. Owners must observe their pets and override the schedule whenever something is off — for example, skip the morning walk if the dog seems lethargic, and call the vet instead.

When in doubt, call the vet. The cost of a phone call is zero. This document is for general orientation only — every pet's "normal" is individual, and your vet knows your pet's history.

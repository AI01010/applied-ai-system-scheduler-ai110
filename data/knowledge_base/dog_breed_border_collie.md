# Border Collie Specific Care

Border Collies are arguably the most demanding companion breed in terms of mental and physical engagement. Bred to work sheep for 8+ hours per day, they do not "settle into apartment life" without a deliberate enrichment plan. Owners who treat a Collie like a Lab usually end up with obsessive shadow-chasing, light-fixation, or compulsive herding of children and joggers.

Daily baseline:

- 06:00 — 45 minute off-leash run or fetch session in a secure area
- 07:00 — Breakfast in a slow feeder or scattered across the lawn
- 09:30 — 10 minute training drill (recall, "look at that" game, place command)
- 13:00 — 20 minute walk plus 5 minutes of trick work
- 16:00 — 30+ minute scent work, agility, or herding-ball session
- 18:00 — Dinner
- 19:30 — Calm chewing or licking activity for 20+ minutes (the "off switch" practice)

Teach an "off switch" early. Collies do not naturally rest — they need to be taught that lying on a mat is the desired default. A 10-minute mat training session daily pays dividends.

Avoid repetitive flashing-light triggers (laser pointers, bike spokes, sunlight on water) which can develop into compulsive disorders in this breed. Replace laser play with scent or food puzzles.

Health watch: Collies are prone to MDR1 gene sensitivity (affecting reactions to certain medications), Collie eye anomaly, and hip dysplasia. Always disclose breed when at the vet so medication choices account for MDR1.

Grooming: brush 2–3 times per week, daily during seasonal shedding. They are double-coated; do not shave them.

This is general breed guidance. Consult your veterinarian for individual health screening and dosing concerns.

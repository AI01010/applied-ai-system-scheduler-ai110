# Conflict: Vet Appointment Collides with Feeding

Vet appointments are usually fixed external constraints — the owner cannot move them. The pet's daily schedule must shift around them.

## Resolution patterns

### Pattern 1 — Routine wellness visit, no fasting required

If the appointment is at 09:00 and breakfast is at 08:00:
- Feed normally at 08:00 (or 30 min earlier if it's a long drive)
- Vet visit at 09:00 — pet is fed, calmer in the waiting room
- Resume normal schedule after

### Pattern 2 — Procedure requiring fasting (surgery, sedation, some imaging)

If fasting is required (typically 8–12 hours pre-procedure):
- Skip the morning feeding entirely
- Provide water until the vet's specified cutoff (often 2 hours before)
- The PawPal+ scheduler should be manually adjusted: mark breakfast complete or delete it for that day
- Plan a soft, small post-procedure meal once the pet is alert (vet will advise)

### Pattern 3 — Annual vaccinations or bloodwork

These typically do not require fasting but the pet may be tired or sore afterward:
- Schedule the heaviest exercise window BEFORE the appointment, not after
- Plan a quiet evening
- Skip vigorous play for 24 hours post-vaccine

## What to bring to the vet (helpful for the AI scheduler indirectly)

- Current medication list (the scheduler tracks these)
- Recent behavioral notes — has the pet been eating less? Sleeping more? The PawPal+ activity log can help here
- A sample if the issue is digestive
- A list of questions

## After the visit

Update the scheduler:
- New medications: add as recurring tasks with the correct cadence
- Changed feeding instructions: edit feeding tasks
- Recheck appointments: schedule the next one immediately so it does not slip

Vet visits are a great prompt to do a yearly schedule audit — pets' needs change with age, and the schedule should evolve with them.

# High-Energy Dog Daily Schedule

High-energy adult dogs — think Border Collies, Belgian Malinois, Vizslas, working-line Labs, and most adolescent dogs aged 8–24 months regardless of breed — need significantly more than the "two walks a day" baseline. Under-exercised high-drive dogs typically present as anxious, destructive, or hypervigilant rather than tired.

A workable daily structure:

- 06:30 — 45–60 minute brisk walk or off-leash run before breakfast
- 07:45 — Breakfast, followed by 30+ minute rest (no exercise post-meal due to bloat risk)
- 09:00 — 10-minute training session: obedience, trick work, or impulse control drills
- 12:30 — Midday potty break with 5 minutes of fetch or tug
- 15:30 — Mental enrichment: snuffle mat, frozen Kong, or puzzle feeder for 20–30 minutes
- 17:30 — Second main exercise window: 30–45 minute walk, ideally with sniffing time and varied terrain
- 19:00 — Dinner
- 20:30 — Calm decompression: chew toy, gentle grooming, or quiet time on a mat

Critical rule: the second exercise window should end before 18:30. Late, intense exercise spikes cortisol and arousal — many "won't settle at bedtime" complaints trace to a 21:00 fetch session.

Variety matters. Rotate sniff-walks, hill walks, swimming, scent work, and structured training so the same 60 minutes does not feel monotonous. A high-energy dog also benefits from a "job" — even a 5-minute "find it" game gives purpose.

Watch for over-exercise in young adolescents: limping, reluctance to walk the next morning, or excessive panting after rest indicate you have pushed too hard. Reduce by 25 percent and consult your vet if signs persist.

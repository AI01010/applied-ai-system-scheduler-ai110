# Schedule for a 9-to-5 Dog Owner

The hardest schedule challenge for working pet owners: the dog is alone 8–9 hours during prime activity hours. The fix is bookending the day with substantial exercise and adding a midday break if at all possible.

Sample weekday schedule (owner works 09:00–17:30):

- 06:30 — Wake; short potty break
- 06:45 — 30–45 min walk before work; longer is better, this is the main exercise window
- 07:30 — Breakfast (after walk so dog is calm during eating)
- 08:00 — Crate or pen time with a frozen Kong or long-lasting chew
- 08:30 — Owner leaves for work
- 12:00 — Dog walker midday visit (30 min walk + potty break) OR owner returns home if possible
- 17:30 — Owner returns; immediate potty break
- 18:00 — Second main exercise window: 30 min walk
- 18:45 — Dinner
- 19:30 — Training session or play (10–15 min)
- 21:30 — Final potty break before bed

Without a midday walker:
- Maximize the morning walk to 60+ minutes
- Use a frozen Kong, slow feeder, or food puzzle for breakfast — slows the meal, adds enrichment
- Consider a slow-release treat dispenser around 12:00 (battery-timed)
- Plan a longer evening walk (45+ min)
- Accept that this is a stretch for high-energy dogs and consider doggy daycare 2x/week

Conflict-aware tips:
- If owner has a 09:00 meeting, scheduling the morning walk for 06:30 builds in a buffer for delays
- Avoid scheduling the evening walk to start at exactly 18:00 if dinner is 18:30 — you need the post-meal rest gap
- The 21:30 potty break must not be skipped, especially for puppies and seniors

This template assumes a healthy adult dog. Puppies, seniors, and high-energy breeds need significant adjustments.

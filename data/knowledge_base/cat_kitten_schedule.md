# Kitten Daily Schedule (8 weeks – 6 months)

Kittens are unrelenting. They have small stomachs, short attention spans, and razor-blade play instincts. A structured schedule turns chaos into predictable cycles.

Sample day:

- 06:30 — Wet food breakfast (small portion — kittens eat 4× daily)
- 07:00 — 15 min wand-toy play
- 07:15 — Brief grooming and inspection
- 09:00 — Independent play / nap
- 11:00 — Second wet meal
- 11:15 — 10 min play
- 13:00 — Quiet time
- 15:00 — Third wet meal + 15 min play
- 17:00 — Litter scoop
- 18:00 — Active play session (longer, 20 min)
- 19:00 — Fourth wet meal (small)
- 19:30 — Wind-down play
- 21:00 — Final litter scoop

Key kitten-specific notes:

- Four meals per day until 4 months, three until 6 months, then transition to adult two-meal schedule
- Never use hands as toys — this teaches biting that is hard to unlearn at adult size
- Socialization window closes around 12 weeks — expose kittens (gently) to many environments, sounds, gentle handling, carrier rides
- Schedule the first vet visit early; second-round vaccinations and parasite checks are time-sensitive
- Spay/neuter typically scheduled around 5–6 months — your vet will advise
- Kittens sleep 18–20 hours a day in irregular bursts — do not panic if your kitten is "always asleep"

Two kittens are dramatically easier than one. They wear each other out and develop healthy social skills. Solo kittens demand far more owner play time.

This is general care guidance; medical and vaccination questions belong with your vet.

# Adult Dog Daily Care (1 to 7 years)

Most adult dogs thrive on two measured meals per day, spaced roughly 10–12 hours apart. A reliable default is 07:00 and 18:00 — this aligns with typical human work schedules and leaves a digestion buffer before bedtime.

Exercise needs vary by breed and individual, but a healthy adult dog of medium energy generally needs 45–90 minutes of activity per day, broken into at least two outings. A practical pattern:

- 07:30 — 20–30 minute morning walk (post-breakfast, after a 30 minute rest)
- 12:30 — 10 minute potty break or yard time
- 17:30 — 30–45 minute walk, sniff-walk, or fetch session
- 21:00 — Final short potty break

Dogs are crepuscular by preference — they tend to be most active in the cooler morning and evening hours and nap mid-day. Honor this rhythm when scheduling.

Mental stimulation matters as much as physical exercise. A 10-minute training session, a snuffle mat at breakfast, or a frozen Kong at 14:00 can prevent the boredom-driven destructive behavior owners often misread as "needing more walks."

Grooming cadence depends on coat: short coats need brushing weekly, double coats need brushing 2–3 times weekly (daily during shedding seasons in spring and fall), and continuously growing coats (poodles, doodles) need professional grooming every 4–6 weeks plus brushing every 2–3 days at home.

Adult dogs should have a dental check at least annually. Daily tooth brushing is the gold standard; a dental chew at 19:00 after dinner is a reasonable minimum.

This is general guidance. Consult your veterinarian for portion sizes, weight management, and breed-specific concerns.

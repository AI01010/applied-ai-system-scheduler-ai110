# Puppy Care Fundamentals (8 weeks to 6 months)

Puppies under six months have small stomachs and rapid metabolisms, so most veterinary nutritionists recommend three to four small meals per day rather than the two-meal adult schedule. A typical puppy day might look like:

- 07:00 — First meal (about 1/4 of daily ration)
- 12:00 — Second meal
- 17:00 — Third meal
- 20:30 — Optional fourth meal for puppies under 12 weeks

Between meals, expect a near-constant cycle of "eat, play, potty, sleep." Puppies sleep 18–20 hours per day; do not interrupt naps for training. After every meal and every nap, take the puppy outside for a potty break within 5–10 minutes — this is the foundation of housetraining.

Exercise should be gentle and short. The common rule of thumb is "5 minutes of structured exercise per month of age, twice a day" — a 4-month-old can handle two 20-minute walks but should not be jogging or doing repetitive jumping until growth plates close around 12–18 months.

Socialization windows close around 14–16 weeks. Schedule short, positive exposures to new people, surfaces, sounds, and friendly vaccinated dogs daily during this period. A 10-minute exposure outing at 16:00 is more valuable than a 60-minute walk.

Grooming should be introduced as a daily 2–3 minute handling routine even if the coat does not need brushing yet — touch paws, ears, and mouth so vet visits and nail trims are tolerated later.

This is general advisory guidance only. Consult your veterinarian for breed-specific feeding amounts, vaccination timing, and any signs of digestive upset or lethargy.

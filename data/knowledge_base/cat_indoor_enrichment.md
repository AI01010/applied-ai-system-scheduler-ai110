# Indoor Cat Enrichment

Indoor cats live longer and safer lives than outdoor cats, but they need owners to actively replace what the environment would otherwise provide: hunting, climbing, scratching, and territorial patrol.

Daily enrichment menu (mix and match — variety matters):

- 10–15 min wand-toy play, ideally twice a day
- A puzzle feeder or treat ball for one meal per day — slows eating and adds problem-solving
- Window perches or "cat TV" (a bird feeder visible from a window)
- Vertical space — at least one cat tree or shelf path; cats use vertical territory to feel safe
- Rotate toys weekly — leaving the same five toys out forever reduces interest

Scratching is non-negotiable. Provide at least one tall sturdy vertical scratcher (sisal-wrapped is ideal) and one horizontal cardboard scratcher per cat. Place near sleeping spots — cats stretch-and-scratch when they wake up.

Multi-cat households need separate resources to prevent stress: one litter box per cat plus one extra, multiple feeding stations, and at least one elevated retreat per cat where others cannot ambush.

Signs of under-enrichment: late-night zoomies, food-bowl barbering (eating compulsively), over-grooming patches, redirected aggression. The fix is almost always more interactive play earlier in the evening, not less food.

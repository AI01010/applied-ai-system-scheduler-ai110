# Kitten Care (8 weeks to 12 months)

Kittens grow at an extraordinary pace and have correspondingly high caloric and developmental needs. Unlike adult cats who can be fed twice daily, kittens under 6 months should eat 3–4 small meals per day to support growth without digestive upset.

A working schedule:

- 07:00 — First meal of kitten-formulated wet or wet+dry food
- 12:00 — Second meal
- 17:00 — Third meal
- 21:00 — Fourth meal (kittens under 4 months)

Use a food specifically labeled "kitten" or "all life stages" — these contain higher protein, fat, and DHA needed for brain and skeletal development. Adult cat food underdelivers on these.

Play is non-negotiable for kittens. Two to four 10–15 minute interactive play sessions per day with a wand toy (not your hands — never your hands) build coordination, drain energy, and prevent the night-time "zoomies" most owners complain about. Best play windows mirror natural hunting times: dawn and dusk.

Socialization window for cats is roughly 2 to 9 weeks — much narrower than dogs. By the time most kittens go to a home (8 weeks), this window is closing. Continue gentle exposure to handling, carrier time, brushing, and visitor traffic during weeks 8–14 to maximize adult friendliness.

Litter training is usually instinctive. Provide one box per cat plus one extra, low-sided for kittens, with unscented clumping litter. Keep it scrupulously clean.

Schedule the first vet visit by 9 weeks for vaccination start, deworming, and weight check. Spay/neuter timing is a vet conversation — common recommendations range from 4 to 6 months.

This is advisory only. Consult your veterinarian for vaccination schedule and growth tracking.

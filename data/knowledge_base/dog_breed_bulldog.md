# English & French Bulldog Care

Bulldogs are brachycephalic — their shortened muzzle compromises efficient cooling and breathing. Care decisions that work fine for a Lab can be dangerous for a Bulldog. The single most important rule: temperature and exertion must be conservatively managed.

Walks should be scheduled around heat:

- 06:30–08:00 — Cool morning walk, 15–20 minutes
- 19:30–21:00 — Cooler evening walk, 15 minutes

Avoid midday outings entirely in summer. Above 24 C / 75 F, or above 21 C / 70 F with high humidity, restrict outdoor activity to brief potty breaks. Heatstroke can develop within minutes in a Bulldog.

Feeding: two measured meals at 08:00 and 18:00. Use a slow-feeder bowl — Bulldogs gulp air and are at elevated risk of bloat and digestive upset. Avoid exercise for 60 minutes after meals.

Snoring, snorting, and reverse sneezing are common. However, persistent gagging, blue-tinged gums, or collapse after mild activity are emergencies. Many Bulldogs benefit from BOAS (brachycephalic obstructive airway syndrome) surgical assessment by a specialist.

Skin folds on the face, tail pocket, and vulva need daily wiping with an unscented pet wipe to prevent yeast and bacterial infections. A 2-minute fold-cleaning routine after the morning walk works well.

Bulldogs typically need 12–14 hours of sleep per day plus several daytime naps. Provide a cool sleeping surface (tile floor, cooling mat) in summer.

Mental enrichment carries most of the engagement load: 10-minute training sessions, snuffle mats at 14:00, and short food puzzles keep them content without overheating.

This is advisory only. A reputable Bulldog-experienced veterinarian is essential for this breed; do not skip routine exams.

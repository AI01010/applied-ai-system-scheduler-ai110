# Owner Traveling — Pet-Sitter Schedule Template

When the primary owner travels, hand the sitter a printed schedule, not just verbal notes. Pets read your stress; clear instructions help everyone.

Sample handoff schedule for a dog and cat:

Morning (sitter arrives 07:00):
- Dog: potty break, 30 min walk, breakfast, fresh water
- Cat: feeding, litter scoop, 5 min play
- Both: medication if on a routine — sitter must initial the chart

Midday (sitter arrives 12:30, optional for solo cats):
- Dog: 20 min walk + bathroom
- Cat: skip if cat tolerates; otherwise 5 min play

Evening (sitter arrives 18:00):
- Dog: 30 min walk, dinner, 15 min play
- Cat: dinner, evening play, litter scoop
- Both: water refresh, brief check for any odd behavior

What to leave for the sitter:
- Vet phone number AND poison control number
- Current medication list with photos of pills
- Address of nearest 24-hour emergency vet
- Authorization to seek medical care up to a stated dollar amount
- Pet's normal eating, drinking, and litter habits described in writing
- Photo of each pet in case of escape
- Microchip ID numbers

Cat-specific note: cats often hide for the first day or two with a sitter. As long as eating, drinking, and using the litter box are happening, hiding is normal. If any of those three stop for over 24 hours, contact the vet.

Dogs: never leave a sitter without specifying which doors the dog can/cannot exit through unleashed, and how the dog responds to delivery people, other dogs, etc.

A 5-minute video walkthrough before you leave is worth more than 5 pages of notes.

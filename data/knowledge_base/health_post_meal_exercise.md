# Post-Meal Exercise Wait Times

Vigorous exercise immediately after a meal increases the risk of GDV (gastric dilatation-volvulus, also known as bloat) — a true emergency where the stomach twists on its axis. Bloat is most common in deep-chested breeds: Great Danes, Standard Poodles, Weimaraners, German Shepherds, Boxers, Setters. Smaller and stockier breeds are at lower but non-zero risk.

Recommended waits between feeding and exercise:

- Light walking (slow pace, no running): 15 minutes
- Normal walk (steady pace): 30 minutes
- Vigorous play, fetch, running: 60 minutes
- Swimming or stair sprints: 90 minutes

Reverse order is safer: exercise FIRST, feed AFTER. A panting, hot dog also should not be allowed to gulp water — let them cool down 10 minutes, then offer water in small portions.

Signs of bloat (call vet immediately, do not wait):
- Distended abdomen, belly looks visibly swollen
- Unproductive retching (trying to vomit, nothing comes up)
- Drooling, restlessness, inability to settle
- Pale gums, weakness

Bloat can kill within hours. If you suspect it, drive to the nearest emergency vet immediately — do not call ahead, do not wait.

This is general safety guidance, not medical advice. If you have specific concerns about your dog's risk factors, talk to your veterinarian.

# Rabbit Basic Daily Care

Pet rabbits need a structured routine — they are crepuscular (most active at dawn and dusk) and creatures of strong habit.

Daily essentials:

- 07:00 — Fresh hay refill (timothy or orchard grass); hay should be 80% of diet and always available
- 07:15 — 1 cup fresh leafy greens per 4 lb of body weight (romaine, cilantro, parsley — never iceberg or onion family)
- 08:00 — Out-of-cage exercise: at least 1 hour, ideally 3+ hours daily; bunny-proof the area first
- 12:00 — Quiet time / nap; rabbits sleep midday
- 17:30 — Second greens portion + small measured pellet portion (1/4 cup per 5 lb body weight, no more)
- 18:00 — Second exercise / bonding session
- 21:00 — Water bottle and hay check before bed

Critical rules:
- Hay is mandatory and must be available 24/7. Without continuous fiber, the gut stalls within 12 hours — a true emergency called GI stasis.
- Pellets are a supplement, not a meal. Adult rabbits get a small fixed amount; growing rabbits and seniors get adjusted portions.
- No grapes, onions, garlic, leeks, raw beans, rhubarb, or iceberg lettuce.
- Litter box training is straightforward — most rabbits naturally pick a corner.

Exercise outside the cage is non-negotiable; a permanently caged rabbit develops bone, muscle, and behavioral problems. A 3 ft x 3 ft cage is the bare minimum even for the smallest breeds.

Annual vet check-ups with a rabbit-savvy vet are recommended. This document is general guidance only — consult your vet for medical questions.

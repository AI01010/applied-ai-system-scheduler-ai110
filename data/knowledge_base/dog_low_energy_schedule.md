# Low-Energy Dog Daily Schedule

Low-energy breeds — Bulldogs, Basset Hounds, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, Mastiffs, many senior dogs of any breed, and brachycephalic (flat-faced) dogs in general — have very different needs than the average high-drive working dog. Pushing them to a 60-minute brisk walk can cause respiratory distress, joint strain, or simply refusal.

A reasonable schedule:

- 08:00 — 15–20 minute leisurely walk, ideally before temperatures climb
- 08:30 — Breakfast
- 12:00 — 10 minute potty walk, light sniffing only
- 16:30 — 15–25 minute walk, slow pace
- 18:00 — Dinner
- 20:30 — 5–10 minute final potty break

Brachycephalic dogs (Pugs, French Bulldogs, English Bulldogs) require special care: walks should never occur when the temperature exceeds 24 C / 75 F or in high humidity. A flat-faced dog panting heavily after 5 minutes is a warning sign — return home and offer water and shade.

Mental enrichment fills the gap left by reduced physical activity. A frozen Lickimat at 14:00, a 5-minute "find the treat" search game, or simple obedience refresh sessions keep low-energy dogs satisfied without exhausting them.

Weight management is the single biggest health risk for low-energy breeds. A bored, lounging Basset who eats every spilled crumb will trend overweight quickly, which compounds joint and breathing issues. Measure portions; do not free-feed.

A common owner mistake is misreading low energy as depression and increasing exercise. If your low-energy dog seems unusually withdrawn, the answer is usually a vet visit, not a longer walk.

This is advisory guidance only. Consult your veterinarian for breed-specific exercise tolerances and weight targets.

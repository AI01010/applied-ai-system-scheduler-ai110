# Adult Cat Daily Care

Adult cats (1–7 years) thrive on a predictable routine. Most do best with two scheduled meals plus access to fresh water. A typical structure:

- 07:00 — Wet food meal (about 1/2 a 5.5oz can per 10lb cat)
- 07:15 — Litter box scoop
- 10:00 — Optional 10–15 minute interactive play (wand toy or laser)
- 13:00 — Quiet time, owner can leave for work
- 18:00 — Wet food meal
- 19:30 — Evening play to mimic the natural hunt-eat-groom-sleep cycle
- 20:00 — Final litter scoop and water refresh

Two meals is the minimum; three smaller meals or controlled grazing on dry food can also work. Avoid free-feeding wet food — it spoils within an hour at room temperature.

Indoor cats need at least 15–30 minutes of structured play daily. Without it, expect "zoomies" at 3am, food-bowl barbering, or aggressive play with hands. Hunt-style play (a wand that moves like prey, with a "kill" at the end) is more satisfying than passive toys.

Adult cats rarely need a bath unless they get into something. Brushing 1–2 times weekly for short-haired cats and daily for long-haired prevents mats and reduces hairballs.

Watch for changes in appetite, water intake, or litter box habits — these are often the first signs something is off. Any sustained change for more than 24–48 hours warrants a vet call.

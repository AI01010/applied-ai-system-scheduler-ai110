# Foods That Are Toxic to Dogs and Cats

The PawPal+ scheduler includes feeding tasks but does not validate ingredient lists. The owner is responsible for what goes in the bowl. This is a quick reference of common household foods that are toxic to pets.

## Universally toxic (never feed)

- Chocolate (any kind, but darker = more dangerous)
- Grapes and raisins (can cause acute kidney failure even in tiny amounts)
- Onions, garlic, leeks, chives (allium family — destroys red blood cells)
- Macadamia nuts (dogs)
- Xylitol (artificial sweetener — found in sugar-free gum, peanut butter, baked goods; causes severe hypoglycemia)
- Alcohol, caffeine
- Avocado (toxic to birds; mostly safe but high-fat for dogs/cats)
- Raw bread dough (yeast expands in stomach, ferments to alcohol)

## Cats specifically

- Lilies (any part of the plant — lethal kidney damage)
- Raw fish in large amounts (thiamine deficiency)
- Cow milk (most cats are lactose intolerant despite the cliché)

## Dogs specifically

- Cooked bones (splinter and cause perforations)
- Fatty scraps (bacon, ham — pancreatitis risk)
- Ibuprofen, acetaminophen, aspirin — never give human painkillers without a vet's specific instruction

## If your pet eats something toxic

- Call your vet OR ASPCA Animal Poison Control: 888-426-4435 (a fee may apply, but the cost is far less than emergency care from a delayed call)
- Do not induce vomiting unless instructed — for some toxins, vomiting makes things worse
- Bring the packaging or a sample of what was eaten to the vet

This document is a general reference, not medical advice. Always call your vet or poison control for specific incidents.

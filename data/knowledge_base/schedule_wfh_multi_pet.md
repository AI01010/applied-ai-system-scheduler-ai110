# Work-from-Home Owner with Multiple Pets

Working from home unlocks more flexibility but introduces a new challenge: pets learn that owner-presence equals attention, and constant interruption becomes the norm. The fix is a structured schedule that gives pets predictable engagement windows so they relax between them.

Sample day for an owner with one dog and one cat, working 09:00–17:00:

- 07:00 — Cat: feeding + 5 min play
- 07:15 — Dog: potty + 30 min walk
- 08:00 — Both pets: feeding (separate spots to avoid resource guarding)
- 09:00 — Work begins; pets settle. Use a long-lasting chew for the dog and a window perch with a sun spot for the cat
- 12:00 — Lunch break: dog gets 15 min walk + bathroom; cat gets 10 min wand-toy play
- 13:00 — Back to work
- 15:30 — Short break: 5 min training session for the dog (sit/down/stay refresh)
- 17:00 — Work ends; immediate dog walk (30 min) — this is the main second exercise window
- 17:45 — Both pets: dinner (separate spots)
- 19:00 — Cat play session (15 min, hunt-style with a wand)
- 20:00 — Family time / training
- 22:00 — Final dog potty break

Multi-pet conflict patterns:
- Feeding at the same time is fine ONLY if pets eat in separate rooms or with a baby gate. Even friendly pets can develop resource guarding.
- The cat's 19:00 play session must come AFTER the dog has been exercised and is settled — otherwise the dog will interrupt.
- Avoid scheduling the dog's walk at the exact moment the cat is being fed; the cat will pace and wait if access changes.

The work-from-home advantage is the midday break. Use it. Pets without a midday outlet often display attention-seeking behavior at 15:00–16:00 — barking at small noises, demanding play during meetings.

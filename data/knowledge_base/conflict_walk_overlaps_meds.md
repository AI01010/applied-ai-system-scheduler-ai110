# Conflict: Walk Time Overlaps with Medication Time

A common conflict: the dog's morning walk is scheduled for 08:00, but a daily medication needs to be given at 08:00 with food.

## Resolution rule

Medication-with-food always wins when there is a hard timing constraint. Shift the walk, not the meds.

Recommended pattern:

- 07:30 — Pre-walk potty break
- 07:35 — Medication with breakfast (food gives the meds something to settle on; reduces nausea)
- 08:30 — Main walk (allows 30+ min digestion before exercise — important for medium and large dogs to reduce bloat risk)

If the medication is "as needed" or has a wide timing window (e.g., once per 24 hours, no specific clock time), do the walk first and give meds after.

## Why the 30-minute post-meal rest matters

Vigorous exercise within 30 minutes of a meal increases bloat risk in deep-chested dogs (Great Danes, Standard Poodles, German Shepherds, Labs to a lesser extent). Bloat (GDV) is a true emergency. The cost of waiting an extra 30 minutes is zero; the cost of skipping it is potentially fatal.

## When the conflict cannot be shifted

Some medications truly must be given at a specific time (e.g., insulin for diabetic dogs, twice daily 12 hours apart). In that case:

- Keep the medication time fixed
- Shift the walk to a non-overlapping window — earlier, or 60+ minutes later
- Use a quick potty break only at the meds-with-food time

This is general guidance only. For any medication-related question, follow your vet's specific instructions — they override schedule-optimization heuristics.

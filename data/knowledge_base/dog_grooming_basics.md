# Dog Grooming Basics

Grooming is more than aesthetics — it catches lumps, parasites, ear infections, and dental issues early. Different coats need different cadences.

## Brushing

- Short, smooth coats (Lab, Pit, Beagle): once a week with a rubber curry or soft bristle
- Double coats (Husky, Golden, Australian Shepherd): 2–3× per week year-round, daily during shed seasons (spring/fall)
- Long coats (Yorkie, Maltese, Shih Tzu): daily — mats develop in 24 hours of neglect
- Wiry coats (Terriers, Schnauzers): weekly with a slicker brush; professional hand-stripping every 8–12 weeks for show coats

## Bathing

- 4–8 weeks between baths for most dogs — over-bathing strips natural oils
- Use dog-specific shampoo (human pH is wrong for dog skin)
- Always brush before bathing to prevent mats from tightening when wet
- Dry thoroughly, especially between toes and in skin folds

## Nails

- Trim every 3–4 weeks; the click on hard floors is your timing cue
- Cut a little at a time to avoid the quick (the blood vessel inside the nail)
- Black nails: trim small slivers and stop when you see a chalky white center

## Ears

- Weekly inspection — should look pink, smell neutral, no waxy buildup
- Floppy-eared breeds (Cocker, Basset) and swimmers need more frequent checks
- Use a vet-approved cleaner; never insert cotton swabs into the ear canal

## Teeth

- Daily brushing is ideal but rare; aim for 3–4× per week minimum
- Dental chews are a supplement, not a replacement
- Annual professional cleanings are the gold standard

## Schedule integration

In PawPal+, set grooming as a "weekly" or "monthly" frequency. Brushing is daily for some breeds, monthly for others. Adjust per the coat type.

This document is general guidance; for skin issues, ear problems, or anything that smells or looks abnormal, consult your veterinarian.

# Pet Parrot Daily Routine

Small to medium parrots (cockatiels, conures, lovebirds, parrotlets) and larger species (Amazons, African Greys, macaws) all need substantial daily interaction. Birds are flock animals — isolation causes feather plucking, screaming, and depression.

Daily structure:

- 07:00 — Cage uncover (birds need 10–12 hours of dark, undisturbed sleep)
- 07:15 — Fresh water refill, food bowl change
- 07:30 — Pellet base diet (60–80%) plus chopped fresh produce (carrots, leafy greens, bell pepper)
- 08:00 — Out-of-cage time: at least 2 hours daily for medium birds, 3+ for large parrots
- 12:00 — Foraging puzzle or toy rotation; hide pellets in shreddable paper for natural foraging
- 16:00 — Second out-of-cage / training session (5 min of clicker training is enriching)
- 18:30 — Light dinner: more produce, small seed treat
- 19:30 — Cage cover for sleep — consistent bedtime is critical for behavioral stability

Forbidden foods: avocado (toxic), chocolate, caffeine, alcohol, onion, garlic, raw beans, fruit pits/seeds (apple seeds especially).

Toys must be rotated weekly — birds destroy them on purpose, that is the point. Plan to replace toys regularly. Foot toys, shreddable paper, foraging puzzles, and bell-style toys all serve different needs.

Signs something is wrong: fluffed feathers all day, sitting on cage floor, sudden silence in a normally vocal bird, change in dropping color/consistency. Any of these warrant a same-day call to an avian vet — birds hide illness until late.

This is general husbandry guidance, not medical advice.

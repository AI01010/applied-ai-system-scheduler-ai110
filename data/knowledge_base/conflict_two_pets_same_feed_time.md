# Conflict: Two Pets Need Feeding at the Same Time

When two or more pets in a household are scheduled for feeding at the same minute (e.g., both at 08:00), you have three workable resolution patterns.

## Pattern 1 — Separate Rooms, Same Time (preferred for most households)

Feed each pet in a different room or behind a baby gate. Same time, no resource competition. Works because most pets do not care about whether a sibling is eating, only that their food bowl is in their normal spot.

This is the recommended default. Add it to the schedule as: "08:00 — Mochi feeding (kitchen) | 08:00 — Luna feeding (bathroom)".

## Pattern 2 — Stagger by 5–10 Minutes (good if rooms are scarce)

Feed the higher-priority pet first (e.g., the one with medication, the one who eats faster, or the one most likely to steal). Feed the second pet in the same area 5–10 minutes later, after the first pet has finished and moved away.

Schedule entry: "08:00 — Mochi feeding | 08:10 — Luna feeding"

## Pattern 3 — Time-Shift One Pet (best for households with one pet on a strict schedule)

If one pet has a medical reason to eat at exactly 08:00 (e.g., medication-with-food, diabetic insulin timing), shift the other pet by 30 minutes.

Schedule entry: "08:00 — Mochi feeding (insulin) | 08:30 — Luna feeding"

## What NOT to do

- Do not feed both pets in the same room without supervision, even if they "get along". Resource guarding can develop suddenly, especially as pets age.
- Do not let one pet finish and walk over to inspect the other's bowl. This teaches the second pet to gulp food too fast (bloat risk in dogs).

The PawPal+ scheduler treats conflicts as warnings, not errors — it is up to the owner to apply one of these patterns. The auto-shift feature in the constraint engine implements Pattern 3 by default.

# Flea and Tick Treatment Timing

Most modern flea and tick preventatives are monthly (oral or topical) or every 8–12 weeks (longer-acting orals). The PawPal+ scheduler treats these as recurring tasks with a "weekly" or "monthly" cadence depending on the product.

General scheduling principles (always defer to your vet's product-specific instructions):

- Oral preventatives: give with a small meal to reduce GI upset
- Topical preventatives: apply between the shoulder blades on dry skin; do not bathe for 48 hours before or after
- Set the recurring task on the same day each month (e.g., the 1st) so you don't lose track
- Sync flea/tick with heartworm prevention if your vet uses a combo product — single date, single reminder

Climate-based notes:

- Year-round prevention is recommended in most of the US, even where winters are cold — many ticks survive freezes and heated indoor spaces enable fleas year-round
- After camping, hiking, or rural travel: do a manual tick check 24–48 hours later in addition to the regular preventative

What this scheduler does NOT do:
- Recommend specific products or dosages
- Track expiration of medication packs (set a separate calendar reminder)
- Replace a vet's judgment

If you suspect your pet has a tick-borne illness (lethargy, joint stiffness, loss of appetite within a few weeks of a tick exposure), contact your veterinarian. This document is general guidance only.
